# 📊 AtliQ Hardware – Sales & Financial Analytics (Excel Business Intelligence Project)
A comprehensive Excel report evaluating the financial performance and sales patterns of Atliq Hardwares.

🔎 Project Background

AtliQ Hardware is a global manufacturer and distributor of PCs, printers, peripherals, and networking products. The company sells through large retail partners and online marketplaces across multiple countries.
The leadership team required a structured analytical solution to replace scattered regional Excel reports. The goal was to consolidate data, analyze performance across dimensions, and generate insights that support better sales and financial decisions.
This project focuses on transforming raw sales data into structured, insight-driven reports using Excel.

🎯 Project Objective
To build a comprehensive Sales and Finance analytics model that:
  - Tracks product, division, customer, and market performance
  - Compares actual sales against targets
  - Evaluates profitability using P&L reports
  - Analyzes Gross Margin trends
  - Highlights growth opportunities and underperforming areas

📈 Sales Analysis

1️⃣ Top 10 Products – Year-over-Year Growth

Identified the top 10 products with the highest sales growth from 2020 to 2021.
This analysis helped:
  - Detect demand shifts
  - Recognize high-growth SKUs
  - Support product-level performance evaluation

2️⃣ Division-Level Revenue Analysis

Compared revenue across divisions:
  - PC Division showed significant growth from 2020 to 2021
  - Accessories & Peripherals recorded the highest revenue expansion
  - Networking & Storage demonstrated steady improvement
This report provided visibility into which business segments contributed most to overall growth.

3️⃣ Product Volume Analysis

  - Identified Top 5 products by quantity sold
  - Identified Bottom 5 products with lowest sales volume
This helped highlight strong-performing products and SKUs that may require pricing, promotion, or portfolio review.

4️⃣ New Product Performance (2021)

Analyzed newly introduced products in 2021 and evaluated their sales contribution.
This helped assess product launch effectiveness and portfolio expansion impact.

5️⃣ Top Revenue-Generating Countries (2021)

Identified the top 5 countries contributing the highest revenue, with India leading the list.
This report supported geographic performance analysis and market prioritization.

💰 Financial Analysis

1️⃣ Customer Net Sales Performance
  
  - Analyzed customer-level revenue trends over multiple years.
  - Observed consistent revenue growth, particularly strong performance in 2021.
This helped evaluate customer contribution and overall sales expansion.

2️⃣ Market Performance vs Sales Targets
  
Compared actual sales against predefined targets across markets.
This analysis:
  - Highlighted regions exceeding targets
  - Identified underperforming markets
  - Supported performance monitoring and corrective planning

3️⃣ Yearly Profit & Loss (P&L) Analysis

Built structured P&L reports at yearly level to analyze:
  - Net Sales
  - Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
  - Gross Margin
  - Profitability trends
This enabled a clearer understanding of financial health over time.

4️⃣ Quarterly Profit & Loss Trends

Performed quarterly comparison to detect seasonal patterns and margin stability.
The analysis showed revenue growth alongside relatively stable margins despite cost changes.

5️⃣ Market-Level Profitability

Developed P&L reports for individual markets to compare:
- Revenue contribution
- Gross margin performance
- Relative profitability
This allowed benchmarking across regions.

6️⃣ Gross Margin % (GM%) Analysis

Analyzed quarterly GM% across sub-zones to identify margin fluctuations and performance consistency.

📌 Key Insights

- Strong revenue growth between 2019–2021
- Accessories & Peripherals emerged as a major revenue driver
- India was the highest revenue-contributing country
- Some markets consistently outperformed targets
- Profit margins varied across regions, highlighting optimization opportunities
- Clear distinction between high-performing and low-performing products
